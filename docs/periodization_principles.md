### 1. Progressive Overload
To induce adaptation, training must gradually become more challenging.
- Increase load (kg)
- Increase volume (sets x reps)
- Increase frequency (per week)
- Decrease rest time (in some cases)

### 2. Specificity
Training adaptations are specific to:
- Movement patterns (e.g., squats vs. hip thrusts)
- Energy systems (aerobic vs. anaerobic)
- Muscle groups

### 3. Recovery & Supercompensation
Improvements occur **between** sessions during recovery.
- Rest days are essential
- Sleep, nutrition, and recovery scores impact adaptation
- Deload weeks allow for recovery before resuming overload
